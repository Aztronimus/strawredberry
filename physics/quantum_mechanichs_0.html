Blackbody radiation

Stefan-Boltzmann law

Total energy emitted per unit of surface of a blackbody.

\begin{align} j^* = \sigma T^4 \end{align}

Wien's displacement law

Peaks of temperature occur at wavelengths that are inversely proportional to temperature.

\begin{align} \lambda_\text{max} = \frac{b}{T} \end{align}

Rayleigh-Jeans law

\begin{align} B_{\nu}(T) = \frac{2 \nu^2 k_B T}{c^2} \end{align}

Wien's distribution law

\begin{align} I_{\nu}(T) = \frac{2 h \nu^2}{c^2}\exp\left(\frac{- h \nu}{k_B T}\right) \end{align}

Planck's distribution law

\begin{align} B_{\nu}(T) = \frac{2 h \nu^3}{c^2}\frac{1}{\exp\left(\dfrac{h \nu}{k_B T}\right) - 1} \end{align}

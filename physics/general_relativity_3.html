Tensors (in matrix notation)

Basis change

Describing one basis in terms of the other \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{c} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \\ \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1}' & m_{1,2}'\\ m_{2,1}' & m_{2,2}'\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} Describing one vector in different basis \begin{align} v &= \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1'\\ v_2'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1'\\ v_2'\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1}' & m_{1,2}'\\ m_{2,1}' & m_{2,2}'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \\ \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1'\\ v_2'\\ \end{array}\right) \end{align}

Scalar product with metric

Metric is how one basis perceive other basis, a basis always perceives itself as cartesian because the components of the basis points perpendicular to each other \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2 \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \\ \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} e_1 \cdot e_1 & e_1 \cdot e_2\\ e_2 \cdot e_1 & e_2 \cdot e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{array}\right) \end{align} But how does it perceives another basis? \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{c} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} e_1' \cdot e_1' & e_1' \cdot e_2'\\ e_2' \cdot e_1' & e_2' \cdot e_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \end{align} Dot product with cartesian metric \begin{align} u \cdot v &= \left(\begin{array}{c} u_1\\ u_2 \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2 \end{array}\right) \end{align} Dot product with a no-cartesian metric \begin{align} u \cdot v &= \left(\begin{array}{c} u_1\\ u_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right) \end{align}

Dual basis

\begin{align} e_i \cdot e^j &= \delta_i^j = \left\lbrace\begin{array}{rcl} 0 & \text{if} & i \neq j\\ 1 & \text{if} & i = j\\ \end{array}\right.\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} e^1\\ e^2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{cc} e_1 \cdot e^1 & e_1 \cdot e^2\\ e_2 \cdot e^1 & e_2 \cdot e^2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T = \left(\begin{array}{c} 1 & 0\\ 0 & 1\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right)^{-1} = \left(\begin{array}{cc} g^{1, 1} & g^{1, 2}\\ g^{2, 1} & g^{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} e^1\\ e^2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} g^{1, 1} & g^{1, 2}\\ g^{2, 1} & g^{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} Describing the same vector in dual basis \begin{align} v &= \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e^1\\ e^2\\ \end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{c} v^1\\ v^2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} g^{1, 1} & g^{1, 2}\\ g^{2, 1} & g^{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{c} v^1\\ v^2\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} g^{1, 1} & g^{1, 2}\\ g^{2, 1} & g^{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} v_1\\ v_2\\ \end{array}\right) \end{align}

New metric with new basis

\begin{align} a &= \left(\begin{array}{c} a_1\\ a_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_1\\ \end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{c} a_1'\\ a_2'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ b &= \left(\begin{array}{c} b_1\\ b_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1\\ e_1\\ \end{array}\right) = \left(\begin{array}{c} b_1'\\ b_2'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} e_1'\\ e_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} a \cdot b &= \left(\begin{array}{c} a_1\\ a_2\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} b_1\\ b_2\\ \end{array}\right)\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{c} a_1'\\ a_2'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1}' & g_{1, 2}'\\ g_{2, 1}' & g_{2, 2}'\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} b_1'\\ b_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ &= \left(\begin{array}{c} a_1'\\ a_2'\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{c} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \left(\begin{array}{c} b_1'\\ b_2'\\ \end{array}\right)\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1}' & g_{1, 2}'\\ g_{2, 1}' & g_{2, 2}'\\ \end{array}\right) &= \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{cc} g_{1, 1} & g_{1, 2}\\ g_{2, 1} & g_{2, 2}\\ \end{array}\right) \left(\begin{array}{cc} m_{1,1} & m_{1,2}\\ m_{2,1} & m_{2,2}\\ \end{array}\right)^T \end{align}

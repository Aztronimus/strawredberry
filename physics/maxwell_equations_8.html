Electromagnetic waves

Linked electric and magnetic fields

If somehow the electric and magnetic field could exist without charges to be created, they would look like this \begin{align} \nabla \cdot \mathbf E &= \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}& \nabla \cdot \mathbf B &= 0\\ \nabla \times \mathbf E &= -\ \frac{\partial \mathbf B}{\partial t}& \nabla \times \mathbf B &= \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial \mathbf E}{\partial t}\\ \end{align} Electric wave \begin{align} \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf E) &= \nabla(\nabla \cdot \mathbf E) - \nabla^2 \mathbf E\\ \nabla \times \left(-\ \frac{\partial \mathbf B}{\partial t}\right) &= - \nabla^2 \mathbf E\\ \frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla \times \mathbf B) &= \nabla^2 \mathbf E\\ \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf E}{\partial t^2} &= \nabla^2 \mathbf E\\ \end{align} Magnetic wave \begin{align} \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf B) &= \nabla(\nabla \cdot \mathbf B) - \nabla^2 \mathbf B\\ \nabla \times \left(\frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial \mathbf E}{\partial t}\right) &= - \nabla^2 \mathbf B\\ \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial}{\partial t} (\nabla \times \mathbf E) &= - \nabla^2 \mathbf B\\ \frac{1}{c^2} \frac{\partial^2 \mathbf B}{\partial t^2} &= \nabla^2 \mathbf B\\ \end{align}

Vector identity

\begin{align} \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf V) &= \nabla(\nabla \cdot \mathbf V) - \nabla^2 \mathbf V\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \mathbf V &= \langle V_x, V_y \rangle\\ \\ \nabla \times \mathbf V &= \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial x}V_y - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}V_x\right) \hat k\\ \nabla \times (\nabla \times \mathbf V) &= \left(\frac{\partial}{\partial x}V_y - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}V_x\right) \hat k\\ &= \left\langle \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y \partial x}V_y - \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2}V_x, \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x \partial y}V_x - \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}V_y \right\rangle\\ \\ \nabla \cdot \mathbf V &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} V_x + \frac{\partial}{\partial y} V_y\\ \nabla(\nabla \cdot \mathbf V) &= \left\langle \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2} V_x + \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x \partial y} V_y, \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y \partial x} V_x + \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2} V_y \right\rangle\\ \\ \nabla^2 \mathbf V &= \left\langle \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}V_x + \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2}V_x, \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}V_y + \frac{\partial^2}{\partial y^2}V_y \right\rangle \end{align}

1D potential well

Infinite potential well

\begin{align} V(x) &= \left\lbrace \begin{array}{lcl} V_0 & \text{if} & x < 0\\ 0 & \text{if} & 0 < x < L\\ V_0 & \text{if} & L < x\\ \end{array} \right.\\ \end{align}

One-dimensional, non-relativistic Schrödinger's equation wave equation.

\begin{align} \frac{- \hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{\partial^2 \Psi(x, t)}{\partial x^2} + V(x) \Psi(x, t) = j \hbar \frac{\partial \Psi(x, t)}{\partial t} \end{align}

One-dimensional, time-independent Schrödinger equation (used to get the stationary states of the system).

\begin{align} \frac{- \hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{d^2 \psi(x)}{dx^2} + V(x) \psi(x) = E \psi(x) \end{align}

Solution

\begin{align} \psi(x) &= e^{a x} & a^2 &= \frac{2m}{\hbar^2} (V(x) - E) \end{align}

We know that \(V_0 > E\)

Considering region I (where \(V(x) = V_0\))

\begin{align} k_1 &= \sqrt{ \frac{2m(V_0 - E)}{\hbar^2} } \end{align} \begin{align} a_1 &= k_1 & a_2 &= -k_1 \end{align} \begin{align} \psi_1(x) &= Ae^{a_1 x} + Be^{a_2 x}\\ &= Ae^{k_1 x} + Be^{- k_1 x}\\ \frac{d\psi_1(x)}{dx} &= A k_1 e^{k_1 x} - B k_1 e^{- k_1 x} \end{align}

Considering region II (where \(V(x) = 0\))

\begin{align} k_2 &= \sqrt{ \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} } \end{align} \begin{align} a_1 &= jk_2 & a_2 &= - jk_2 \end{align} \begin{align} \psi_2(x) &= C \cos(k_2 x) + D \sin(k_2 x)\\ \frac{d\psi_2(x)}{dx} &= - C \sin(k_2 x) + D \cos(k_2 x) \end{align}

Considering region III (where \(V(x) = V_0\))

\begin{align} k_1 &= \sqrt{ \frac{2m(V_0 - E)}{\hbar^2} } \end{align} \begin{align} a_1 &= k_1 & a_2 &= -k_1 \end{align} \begin{align} \psi_3(x) &= Ee^{a_1 x} + Fe^{a_2 x}\\ &= Ee^{k_1 x} + Fe^{- k_1 x}\\ \frac{d\psi_3(x)}{dx} &= E k_1 e^{k_1 x} - F k_1 e^{- k_1 x} \end{align}

In order to preserve normalization

\begin{align} B &= 0\\ E &= 0 \end{align}

Boundary conditions

\begin{align} \psi_1(0) &= \psi_2(0)\\ A &= C\\ \\ \left.\frac{d\psi_1}{dx}\right|_{x = 0} &= \left.\frac{d\psi_2}{dx}\right|_{x = 0}\\ A k_1 &= D\\ \\ \psi_2(L) &= \psi_3(L)\\ C \cos(k_2 L) + D \sin(k_2 L) &= Fe^{- k_1 L}\\ \\ \left.\frac{d\psi_2}{dx}\right|_{x = L} &= \left.\frac{d\psi_3}{dx}\right|_{x = L}\\ - C \sin(k_2 L) + D \cos(k_2 L) &= - F k_1 e^{- k_1 L} \end{align}

Expressions

\begin{align} C (k_1 \cos(k_2 L) - \sin(k_2 L)) + D (k_1 \sin(k_2 L) + \cos(k_2 L)) &= 0\\ \end{align} \begin{align} 2k_1 \cos(k_2 L) + (k_1^2 - 1) \sin(k_2 L) &= 0\\ \end{align} \begin{align} k_1^2 + 2k_1 \cot(k_2 L) - 1 &= 0\\ \end{align}

Taking on count the following relation, one could create a \(k_1\) vs. \(k_2\) space

\begin{align} k_1^2 + k_2^2 &= \frac{2mV_0}{\hbar^2} \end{align} \begin{align} k_1 &= \pm \sqrt{\frac{2mV_0}{\hbar^2} - k_2^2} = - \cot(k_2 L) \pm |\csc(k_2 L)| \end{align}

The latter expression can be simplified, in order to ignore relations with \(k_1 < 0\)

\begin{align} k_1 &= \sqrt{\frac{2mV_0}{\hbar^2} - k_2^2} = - \cot(k_2 L) + |\csc(k_2 L)| \end{align}

2D potential well

Schrödinger equation

\begin{align} \frac{- \hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \left(\frac{\partial^2 \psi(x, y)}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 \psi(x, y)}{\partial y^2}\right) + V(x, y) \psi(x, y) = E \psi(x, y) \end{align}

Separation of variables

\begin{align} \psi(x, y) &= \psi_x(x) \psi_y(y)\\ V(x, y) &= V_x(x) + V_y(y) \end{align}

Rearranging the equation is as follows:

\begin{align} V_x(x) - \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{\psi_x(x)} \cdot \frac{\partial^2 \psi_x(x)}{\partial x^2} + V_y(y) - \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{\psi_y(y)} \cdot \frac{\partial^2 \psi_y(y)}{\partial y^2} = E \end{align} \begin{align} E_x + E_y &= E \end{align}

We get the following equations

\begin{align} \frac{- \hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{\partial^2 \psi_x(x)}{\partial x^2} + V_x(x) \psi_x(x) &= E_x \psi_x(x) \end{align} \begin{align} \frac{- \hbar^2}{2m} \cdot \frac{\partial^2 \psi_y(y)}{\partial y^2} + V_y(y) \psi_y(y) &= E_y \psi_y(y) \end{align}

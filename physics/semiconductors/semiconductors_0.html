Kronig-Penney Model

Model

Periodic Potential function

\begin{align} V(x) &= \left\lbrace \begin{array}{lcl} 0 & \text{if} & 0 < x < a\\ V_0 & \text{if} & -b < x < 0\\ \end{array} \right.\\ \\ V(x) &= V(x + a + b) \end{align}

Bloch theorem.

\begin{align} \psi(x) &= u(x)e^{jkx}\\ \psi(x, t) &= \psi(x)\phi(t) = u(x)e^{jkx} \cdot e^{-j(E/\hbar)t}\\ \psi(x, t) &= u(x)e^{j(kx-(E/\hbar)t)}\\ \end{align}

Time-independent Schrödinger equation (used to get the stationary states of the system).

\begin{align} \frac{\partial^2 \psi(x, t)}{\partial x^2} + \gamma^2\psi(x, t) = 0 \end{align} \begin{align} \gamma^2 &= \frac{2m}{\hbar^2}(E - V(x)) \end{align}

Solution

Substituting in Schrödinger equation.

\begin{align} \frac{\partial \psi(x, t)}{\partial x} &= \frac{\partial}{\partial x} (\psi(x) \phi(t)) = \frac{d\psi(x)}{dx} \phi(t)\\ \\ \frac{d\psi(x)}{dx} &= \frac{d}{dx} \left(u(x)e^{jkx}\right)\\ &= \frac{du(x)}{dx}e^{jkx} + u(x)\frac{de^{jkx}}{dx}\\ &= \frac{du(x)}{dx}e^{jkx} + u(x)e^{jkx}jk\\ &= e^{jkx}\left(\frac{du(x)}{dx} + u(x)jk \right)\\ \\ \frac{d^2 \psi(x)}{dx^2} &= \frac{d}{dx} \left( e^{jkx}\left(\frac{du(x)}{dx} + u(x)jk \right) \right)\\ &= \frac{de^{jkx}}{dx} \left( \frac{du(x)}{dx} + u(x)jk \right) + e^{jkx} \frac{d}{dx} \left( \frac{du(x)}{dx} + u(x)jk \right)\\ &= e^{jkx} jk \left( \frac{du(x)}{dx} + u(x)jk \right) + e^{jkx} \left( \frac{d^2 u(x)}{dx^2} + \frac{du(x)}{dx}jk \right)\\ &= e^{jkx} \left( 2jk \frac{du(x)}{dx} - k^2 u(x) + \frac{d^2 u(x)}{dx^2} \right)\\ \\ \frac{\partial^2 \psi(x, t)}{\partial x^2} &= \frac{d^2\psi(x)}{dx^2} \phi(t)\\ \\ &= \left( 2jk \frac{du(x)}{dx} - k^2 u(x) + \frac{d^2 u(x)}{dx^2} \right) e^{jkx} \phi(t) \end{align}

We finally get:

\begin{align} &\frac{\partial^2 \psi(x, t)}{\partial x^2} + \gamma^2\psi(x, t) = 0\\ &\left( 2jk \frac{du(x)}{dx} - k^2 u(x) + \frac{d^2 u(x)}{dx^2} \right) e^{jkx} \phi(t) + \gamma^2 u(x) e^{jkx} \phi(t) = 0\\ &\frac{d^2 u(x)}{dx^2} + 2jk \frac{du(x)}{dx} + \left(\gamma^2 - k^2 \right) u(x) = 0\\ \end{align}

Solving this differential equation gives us:

\begin{align} u(x) &= Ee^{j\left(\gamma - k\right)x} + Fe^{- j\left(\gamma + k\right)x}\\ &= \left( Ee^{j\gamma x} + Fe^{- j\gamma x} \right) e^{-jkx} \end{align}

Considering that \(\gamma\) as a constant in regions I and II (not in the borders), we get:

\begin{align} \frac{du(x)}{dx} &= j \left( \gamma \left( Ee^{j \gamma x} - Fe^{- j \gamma x} \right) - k \left(Ee^{j \gamma x} + Fe^{- j \gamma x} \right) \right) e^{-jkx}\\ &= j \left( E (\gamma - k) e^{j \gamma x} - F (\gamma + k) e^{- j \gamma x} \right) e^{-jkx}\\ \end{align}

Considering region I (where \(V(x) = 0\)):

\begin{align} \frac{d^2 u_1(x)}{dx^2} + 2jk \frac{du_1(x)}{dx} + (\alpha^2 - k^2) u_1(x) = 0 \end{align} \begin{align} u_1(x) &= \left(Ae^{j \alpha x} + Be^{- j \alpha x}\right) e^{-jkx}\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \frac{du_1(x)}{dx} &= j \left( A (\alpha - k) e^{j \alpha x} - B (\alpha + k) e^{- j \alpha x} \right) e^{-jkx}\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \alpha^2 = \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} \end{align} Considering region II (where \(V(x) = V_0\)): \begin{align} \frac{d^2 u_2(x)}{dx^2} + 2jk \frac{du_2(x)}{dx} + \left(\beta^2 - k^2 \right) u_2(x) = 0\\ \end{align} \begin{align} u_2(x) &= \left( Ce^{ j \beta x} + De^{- j \beta x} \right) e^{-jkx} \end{align} \begin{align} \frac{du_2(x)}{dx} &= j \left( C (\beta - k) e^{j \beta x} - D (\beta + k) e^{- j \beta x} \right) e^{-jkx}\\ \end{align} \begin{align} \beta^2 &= \frac{2m}{\hbar^2}(E - V_0) \end{align}

Cauchy boundary conditions

\begin{align} u_1(0) &= u_2(0)\\ A + B &= C + D \end{align} \begin{align} \left.\frac{du_1}{dx}\right|_{x = 0} &= \left.\frac{du_2}{dx}\right|_{x = 0}\\ A (\alpha - k) - B (\alpha + k) &= C (\beta - k) - D (\beta + k) \end{align} \begin{align} u_1(a) &= u_2(-b)\\ Ae^{j\left(\alpha - k\right)a} + Be^{- j\left(\alpha + k\right)a} &= Ce^{-j\left(\beta - k\right)b} + De^{j\left(\beta + k\right)b} \end{align} \begin{align} \left.\frac{du_1}{dx}\right|_{x = a} &= \left.\frac{du_2}{dx}\right|_{x = -b}\\ A (\alpha - k) e^{j (\alpha - k) a} - B (\alpha + k) e^{- j (\alpha + k) a} &= C (\beta - k) e^{- j (\beta - k) b} - D (\beta + k) e^{j (\beta + k) b} \end{align}

Determinant

\begin{align} \left(\begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 1 & - 1 & - 1\\ \alpha - k & - (\alpha + k) & - (\beta - k) & \beta + k\\ e^{j\left(\alpha - k\right)a} & e^{- j\left(\alpha + k\right)a} & - e^{-j\left(\beta - k\right)b} & - e^{j\left(\beta + k\right)b}\\ (\alpha - k) e^{j (\alpha - k) a} & - (\alpha + k) e^{- j (\alpha + k) a} & - (\beta - k) e^{- j (\beta - k) b} & (\beta + k) e^{j (\beta + k) b} \end{array}\right) \end{align}

By Cramer's rule, the determinant of a \(4 \times 4\) matrix is:

\begin{align} \left|\begin{array}{cccc} a & b & c & d\\ e & f & g & h\\ i & j & k & l\\ m & n & o & p \end{array}\right| &= a\cdot\left|\begin{array}{cccc} f & g & h\\ j & k & l\\ n & o & p \end{array}\right| - b\cdot\left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & g & h\\ i & k & l\\ m & o & p \end{array}\right| + c\cdot\left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & f & h\\ i & j & l\\ m & n & p \end{array}\right| - d\cdot\left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & f & g\\ i & j & k\\ m & n & o \end{array}\right|\\ \\ \left|\begin{array}{cccc} f & g & h\\ j & k & l\\ n & o & p \end{array}\right| &= fkp + joh + ngl - nkh - jgp - fol\\ \left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & g & h\\ i & k & l\\ m & o & p \end{array}\right| &= ekp + ioh + mgl - mkh - igp - eol\\ \left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & f & h\\ i & j & l\\ m & n & p \end{array}\right| &= ejp + inh + mfl - mjh - ifp - enl\\ \left|\begin{array}{cccc} e & f & g\\ i & j & k\\ m & n & o \end{array}\right| &= ejo + ing + mfk - mjg - ifo - enk\\ \\ &=afkp + ajoh + angl - ankh - ajgp - afol\\ &+bmkh + bigp + beol - bekp - bioh - bmgl\\ &+cejp + cinh + cmfl - cmjh - cifp - cenl\\ &+dmjg + difo + denk - dejo - ding - dmfk\\ \end{align}
